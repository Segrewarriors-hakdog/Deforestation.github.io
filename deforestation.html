DEFORESTATION

Solution Summary

The Philippines is one of the most severely deforested countries in the tropics and most deforestation has happened in the last 40 years. Estimates place forest cover in the Philippines in the year 1900 at 21 million hectares, covering 70 % of the total land area.

Our solution is campaign. We chose campaign to interact easily with our target participants. Our solution contains the definition and some causes and effects of deforestation. We will also explain the other contents of our campaign for them to understand it. We think that it will be effective because we will be able to explain clearly our problem to them.

In accordance with Wikipedia, deforestation is the conversion of forested areas to non-forest land use such as arable land, urban use, logged area of wasteland.

CAMPAIGN

How does the solution work?

We will first introduce our topic to our target participants. After that, we will tell them our problem and we will also explain why we chose that problem. For our opening remarks, we will give them idea about our topic and after that, we will now discuss our topic and after that, we will discuss some effects of deforestation and we will also explain it to them. Then last one is our closing remarks wherein we're going to ask them questions about the things they've learned and we will give him/her a small price if he/she will answer the question correctly.

FEATURES

BENEFITS

EARLY PROTOTYPE

USABILITY TEST

A. Pre- testing

1. What do you know about deforestation?

2. Are you aware of the causes of deforestation?

3. Are you aware of the effects of doforestation?

4. What are your possible solutions of deforestation?

5. As a student, how can you prevent deforestation?

B. Testing

1. Do you think that our campaign will help our target participants to learn more about deforestation?

2. Which part of our campaign seems to be interesting?

3. Which part of our campaign needs an improvement?

4. Did you clearly understand the message of our presentation?

5. Did we achieved your expectations in our campaign?

C. Post- testing

1. What are your feedbacks on our campaign?

2. Did our presentation convinced you to prevent the risks of deforestation?

3. What can you say to the overall appearance of the webpage?

4. What can you suggest to make our presentation better?

5. Did we achieved your expectations in our mini-seminar?

USABILITY TEST RESULT

For the pre-testing part, question 1, most of them said that deforestation is about cutting trees illegaly. For question 2, 4 out of 5 members of the group said that they're not aware of the causes of deforestation. While 1 out of 5 members of the group said that he/she is aware of the causes of deforestation.For question 3,5 out of 5 members of the group said that they are aware of the effects of deforestation. For question 4, most of the members of the group said that the possible solution of deforestation is to stop cutting trees, some of them said that the possible solution is to make a commercial about the the problem which is deforestation. For question 5, all of the members of the group said taking care of the trees can be a possible solution to prevent deforestation.

For the Testing part, question 1, 5 out of 5 members of the group said that our campaign will help our target participants to learn more about deforestation. For question 2, most of our target participants said that the most interesting part of our campaign is our opening remarks. For question 3, 5/5 of our target participants said that the closing remarks of our campaign needs more improvement.

For the Post Testing part,most of our target participants said that they liked the designs in our slides. for question 2, all of our target participants got convinced to prevent the risks of deforestation. For question 3, most of our target participants said that the overall appearance of our webpage is good. For question 4,most of them said that we must have more energy to make our presentation better. For question 5, some of them said that in every donations, we should plant a tree.
